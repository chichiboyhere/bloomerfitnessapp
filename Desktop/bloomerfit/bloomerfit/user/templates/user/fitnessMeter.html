The fitness meter, as I call it, is actually a combination of your body mass index(BMI) and your waist-hip ratio(WHR). The essence of the BMI and WHR is to decide, using a few parameters which I shall unveil shortly, the level of fat and its distribution in the body. Excess body fat, particularly upper body fat has been medically proven to be the leading cause of such medical issues as hypertension, heart attack, obesity and so on. Therefore it is important to keep the body fat moderate or low in order to stay healthy. A combination of lifestyle-related factors can help one in achieving the desired body fat level, and thus, maintain a healthy lifestyle.Regular exercise, balanced diets eaten in adequate quantities and in good time, proper rest, avoidance of harmful habits like smoking or excessive drinking, steering clear of drug abuse are some of the ways to maintain both mental and physical health.

Using a combination of BMI and WHR one can test their fitness level via body fat level. Here's how that works. First of all what really is BMI and WHR? Well BMI(short for: body mass index) is the the ratio of a person's mass(in kilogram) to the square of their height(in meter-squared). If the BMI result is less than 19, it means the person is underweight. A BMI between 19 and 24(inclusive) indicates a healthy weight and then a BMI running from 25 to 29 indicates overweight, while a BMI from 30 and above is considered obese and thus poses a threat to one's health.

Of course the BMI has it's lapses. For instance a person's BMI result might be high, indicating signs of high body fat, when actually the person has a moderate to low body fat. A good example is that of body builders who have well-developed and toned muscles. Now muscles have a greater density than fat, meaning it is possible to have a lean person with a high weight and as such high BMI. For this reason, we need a second parameter to clarify things. This is where the WHR(waist-hip ratio) comes into play. The WHR is simply the ratio of the waist versus the hips, i.e divide the measurement of the circumference of your waist by the circumference of your hips. Research as shown that the WHR for women not pregnant should not exceed 0.85, values higher are not good for health. The ideal WHR for women is 0.7. For men an ideal WHR is 0.9. So as you can see, women generally have a lower WHR than men due to their generally bigger hip bones, necessary for the support of foetus during pregnancy.

So there you have it, with a good combination of your BMI and WHR, it could be decided whether or not you are in a good physical shape. So let's measure our BMI-WHR values, shall we? Get on the scale and get your mass in kilogram. Also get your height measurement, from your head to your toes, preferably without your shoes on. Next get a tape rule and measure your waist round. For best results ensure you are in a standing position with your tape-rule going round the part of your belly that tappers the most, just right above your navel. Also make sure you are relaxed, without sucking in your belly and pushing it out. Make sure the rule is horizontal, not sagging down, but flat against your skin. Do the same for the hips, ensuring your clothing is right next to your skin and the measurement is around your buttocks, where it's widest as shown in the pictures below.

Now armed with your mass, your height(in cm), your waist measurement and hips measurement(both in inches or both in centimeters, not mixed), you can try out your BMI and WHR and see just how physically fit you are.
