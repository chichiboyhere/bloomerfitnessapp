Like every other journey or set goals in life, your journey towards a healthy lifestyle will not be easy. In the end it all comes down to how badly you want it. It goes without saying that there would be sacrifices. You'd have to let go of some unhealthy lifestyle, while adopting healthy ones. The problem a lot of us have with imbibing a healthier life routine is the lack of motivation. Why do I have to go through the stringent, regimented lifestyle? Who am I trying to impress with it? What do I stand to gain from such sacrifices? Are the gains worth the pleasures I have to sacrifice? These and lots more are the questions we often have to comfront as we make the decision for a healthier lifestyle.

To some of us, these questions have easy answers? Consider an athelete versus a sedentary person(e.g someone like a secretary, banker, ... whose job does not require a lot moving around.) While the athlete knows he has no choice than to be fit through eating healthy meals and working out on a daily basis, while steering clear of hard drugs and other harmful substances in order to attain optimal performance in his career, the everyday Joe with a sedentary lifestyle does not have a similar motvation. So then where does our non-athelete get his motivation to adopt a healthy lifestyle?

Well to put it succinctly, he/she has a prolonged life in good health as a motivation. If you want to eliminate or at least reduce a visit to the hospital on health reasons then you have no choice than to adopt a healthy lifestyle. It's that simple. More so, if you desire being more physically attractive than the majority of the population, adopting a healthy lifestyle should come as second nature.

The good news on this fitness journey is that, as with most things in life, it can soon become second nature. And your body starts craving healthier options than harmful ones after you have initially subjected yourself to the initial rigors that come with adopting a healthy lifestyle.

Here are some healthy life choices that you would have to make to become fit and fine:

  • Healthy Food and fruits eaten in moderation and on time (breafast at 8am, lunch and super to follow at intervals of 4/5 hours)
  • As much as possible, your last meal of the day should come at least 2 hours before bed time. This allows the meal to have left your stomach before going to bed, thus avoiding weight gain in the abdominal region.
  • Choose fish over meat. If you must take meat choose lean meat or offals over fatty or red meat
  • Choose veggies and natural organic fruits over canned drinks and fruit juice. The former have helpful digestive fibre(roughage) that has been removed from the latter.
  • Regular exercise(10 minutes of daily rigorous exercises like Burpee, running and other cardios or 30 minutes of less rigorous exercises like walking done on a daily basis)
  • Moderate intake of alcohol – 14 units(that is 140 ml) of pure alcohol per week(this is equivalent to taking 4 bottles of beer with a percentage of about 6-7% of alcohol in it within a week.) And you have to be of legal age to even contemplate drinking alcohol.
  • Practice complete abstinence from smoking (there's no such thing as smoking in moderation)
  • Steer clear of drugs that have not been recommended by your doctor
  • Sleep 6 to 8 hours daily, particularly at night. Ever heard of 'beauty sleeep'?(winks)
If you keep to these rules, even to 80% adherence, for three months, you will notice a remarkable difference in your fitness level and overall body physique.
